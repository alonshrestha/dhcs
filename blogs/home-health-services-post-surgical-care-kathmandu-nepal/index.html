Home Health Services for Post-Surgical Care in Kathmandu, Nepal

Recovering from surgery is not the same for everyone. It can be a challenging experience, or a smooth sail depending upon the combination of medical care, rest, and lifestyle adjustments one can achieve.

While hospitals provide immediate post-surgical care, many patients are discharged before they have fully recovered. Post-surgical care at home by untrained individuals can be risky and also challenging in some cases. This is where home health service providers like Doctors House Call Service Nepal can play a crucial role to make the recovery as smooth as possible.

Home visits offer personalized care, reduce hospital readmission rates, and improve overall patient well-being. Here are some of the benefits of home health service for post-surgical recovery:

1. Personalized Care and Comfort

Every patient is different and every patient needs equal attention which is not guaranteed in hospitals where the healthcare professionals have to handle many patients. Being in the comfort of your home, a patient is able to feel relaxed with a familiar environment and their family to support them all the time.

Hospitals also have some hectic environments and can be noisy to share wards with 10-20 patients along with their visitors. It could be stressful for a patient, which might affect their recovery. Being at home with their loved ones provides a sense of security and really puts the patient at ease during their recovery phase.

Home health serviceâ€™s main agenda is to provide the personalized care that you need. The healthcare professionals may suggest all the required home adjustments for the comfort of the patient and help create that comfortable environment.

2. Reduced Risk of Infections

Patients recovering from surgery are often vulnerable to infections due to weakened immune systems. Hospitals, despite their strict sanitation measures, are environments where infections can spread easily. With the help of healthcare at home, patients minimize their exposure to hospital-acquired infections (HAIs), significantly reducing the risk of complications.

This hands-on care helps to detect early signs of infection and take prompt action, preventing severe complications that might require hospital readmission.

3. Medication Management and Adherence

Post-surgical recovery includes various types of medications that a patient has to consume on a timely basis. A home healthcare provider can ensure that the patient has been taking their medications as they should on time.

There might be some cases where the patients take the wrong medication or incorrect dosage of a medication. Having a personal doctor and nurses arrive at your home to take care of you and ensure safety helps the patient and the family members relieved from the stress of potential harm.

4. Pain Management and Rehabilitation Support

Pain management is another aspect of post-surgical recovery that may be missed when recovering at home. Delayed healing, uncontrolled pain, etc., may hinder the progress of the recovery and often be misunderstood to be normal. A personalized healthcare provider is able to communicate efficiently with the patient and the family to identify any type of unnatural feeling of unease.

Having a physiotherapist come home on time and help you in your rehabilitation journey helps save time traveling for the patient and the family. It saves the hassle of transportation moving back and forth for therapy.

5. Emotional and Psychological Support

Surgery does not affect only physically but also mentally. Patients may experience anxiety, depression, or feelings of isolation during the recovery phase. Home visits provide an opportunity for healthcare professionals to offer emotional support, address concerns, and educate patients about the recovery process. Being at your own home also allows patients to be with their family, which eases their mind.

Having a trusted individual who takes care of you and comforts you every day helps as the patient doesn't have to be anxious about the other person. House visits by the doctors allow the patients to build a strong sense of trust towards them which helps them cope with stress and uncertainty.

6. Education and Guidance for Patients and Caregivers

Post-surgical recovery is not always an easy hurdle to pass as a patient and their caregivers. Many cases have been such that the caregivers do not fully understand the instructions provided to them. This can increase the risk of delayed recovery or maybe an infection that might worsen the case.

To have a professional trained to carry out all of these tasks ensuring your safety makes this hurdle comfortable to pass through. They can also help the caregivers and patients to remind them about dos and don'ts during and post-recovery phase.

7. Encouraging Independence and Confidence

A structured recovery plan with home visits encourages patients to regain independence at their own pace. Healthcare professionals can assist patients in setting realistic goals for mobility and daily activities, fostering a sense of progress and achievement.

For elderly or disabled patients, home visits can ensure that they are regaining function at a safe pace while providing necessary support. As patients become more self-sufficient, their confidence in managing their own health improves, leading to better long-term outcomes.

8. Reduced Healthcare Costs and Hospital Readmissions

Home visits can help reduce the overall cost of healthcare by minimizing the need for hospital readmissions and emergency room visits, especially in Kathmandu.

Minor recurring issues like fever can be easily treated with a health professional visiting your home that really saves money, time, and lets the patient be at ease as much as possible.

Conclusion

Home visits from a home healthcare provider are almost equivalent to bringing hospital services at home.

In some cases, maybe even better for the mental health of the patient, which definitely helps recover a patient faster.
