Dengue Care at Your Home in Nepal

Each year, during the rainy season, Nepal faces a significant health crisis with dengue fever, affecting thousands and, tragically, leading to loss of life for some.

Historical data shows that during peak periods, the number of dengue patients can surge so dramatically that hospitals often struggle to accommodate everyone, facing shortages of resources like beds, medical staff, and other essential supplies.

This can delay treatment and increase the risk of complications for patients.

As a home healthcare service provider in Nepal, we understand the difficulties and challenges families face during this challenging time.

Our mission is to deliver effective, high-quality care to patients in the comfort of their own homes.

What is Dengue?

Dengue is a viral disease transmitted through the bite of infected mosquitoes, leading to a range of illnesses that can significantly impact health. This can lead to serious health issues if not treated properly.

Some symptoms of dengue are:

  • High fever
  • Severe headaches
  • Joint and muscle pain
  • Skin rash
  • Nausea and vomiting

These symptoms usually appear four to seven days after being bitten, and dengue is not transmitted from the affected person.
